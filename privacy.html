Last updated:

Overview

ImageSaver is a Chrome browser extension that allows users to save images from web pages and optionally create Pinterest pins from those images. This policy explains what data the extension handles, how it is used, and your rights as a user.

We do not operate any servers. All data stays in your browser unless you explicitly choose to send it to Pinterest via the Pinterest API.

Data We Handle

When you use ImageSaver, the extension temporarily processes:

  • The URL of images you click on web pages
  • The URL of the web page the image was found on
  • Your Pinterest OAuth access token (only if you connect Pinterest)
  • Pinterest board names fetched from your account (only if you connect Pinterest)
  • Pin descriptions and tags you type into the extension

How We Use Your Data

  • Image and page URLs are stored locally in your browser using chrome.storage.local so the last captured image persists when you reopen the panel
  • Your Pinterest OAuth token is stored locally so you do not have to log in on every use
  • Image URLs, page URLs, descriptions, and tags are sent directly to Pinterest's API only when you click "Pin It!" — they are not sent anywhere else
  • We do not collect, transmit, or store any data on our own servers
  • We do not use any analytics, tracking, or advertising tools

Third Party Services

This extension integrates with the Pinterest API to create pins on your behalf. When you connect your Pinterest account, you are subject to Pinterest's own Privacy Policy and Terms of Service. ImageSaver only requests the minimum permissions needed: pins:write and boards:read.

Data Retention & Deletion

  • All locally stored data (saved image, page URL, Pinterest token) can be cleared at any time by clicking the ↻ refresh button in the extension
  • You can fully remove all extension data by uninstalling ImageSaver from Chrome
  • You can revoke Pinterest access at any time by going to your Pinterest Security Settings and removing the ImageSaver app
